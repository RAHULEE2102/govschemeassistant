Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY)

PMAY helps poor and middle-income families build or buy a house with direct financial support or home loan interest subsidy. Covers both urban (PMAY-U) and rural (PMAY-G) citizens.

Simple Overview

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is the government's flagship housing scheme to provide affordable housing to all eligible citizens. PMAY-Gramin gives cash assistance to build a home in villages. PMAY-Urban offers interest subsidies on home loans for city dwellers. The house must be registered in the woman's name (or jointly).

Who Can Apply?

  • Families who do not own a pucca (permanent) house anywhere in India
  • Families who have never received housing benefits from any previous government scheme
  • PMAY-Gramin: Families listed in the SECC-2011 database
  • PMAY-Urban: EWS, LIG, MIG-I, and MIG-II families based on income

Income Limits

EWS: up to ₹3 lakh | LIG: ₹3–6 lakh | MIG-I: ₹6–12 lakh | MIG-II: ₹12–18 lakh

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card (all adult family members)
  • Income certificate or salary proof
  • Declaration of no pucca house in family name
  • Bank account passbook
  • Affidavit of no previous housing scheme benefit
  • SC/ST/OBC certificate if applicable

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    PMAY-Gramin: Check SECC list

    Visit pmayg.nic.in to check if your family is in the SECC-2011 beneficiary list. If not, approach your Gram Panchayat for the waiting list.

  2. 2
    PMAY-Gramin: Contact Gram Panchayat

    The Gram Pradhan or Panchayat Secretary will guide you through the local registration process.

  3. 3
    PMAY-Urban: Visit pmaymis.gov.in

    Click 'Citizen Assessment' and enter your Aadhaar to start the urban application.

  4. 4
    Select the correct component

    Choose Beneficiary Led Construction (to build on your own land) or Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (for home loan applicants).

  5. 5
    Complete Aadhaar verification

    Your Aadhaar must be seeded with your bank account. Without this, instalments will be blocked.

  6. 6
    Receive money in stages

    For Gramin, money is released in stages as construction is completed and verified through geotagged photographs on the Awaas App.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • PMAY-Gramin: ₹1.2 lakh (plains) or ₹1.3 lakh (hilly areas) for house construction
  • PMAY-Urban: Interest subsidy of 3–6.5% on home loans — saving up to ₹2.67 lakh
  • Additional ₹12,000 for toilet construction under SBM
  • 90–95 days of MGNREGA wages for unskilled labour in house construction
  • Priority for women, SC/ST, minorities, and differently-abled persons

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not checking SECC list before applying for PMAY-Gramin
  • Applying for multiple housing schemes simultaneously — causes automatic rejection
  • Not completing Aadhaar-bank linkage — delays all instalments
  • Starting construction before receiving the first instalment
  • Submitting false declarations about not owning a house — this is a criminal offence

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I live in a rented house in a city. Can I apply for PMAY-Urban?
Yes, if your household income is within the eligible range, you don't own a pucca house anywhere in India, and haven't previously received government housing assistance.
Q: My name isn't in the SECC list. Can I still get PMAY-Gramin?
Your Gram Panchayat maintains a waiting list for eligible families not on the SECC list. You can request inclusion. Approvals happen as funds become available.
Q: Must the house be in a woman's name?
Under PMAY-Gramin it is mandatory to register the house in the woman's name, or jointly with her husband.

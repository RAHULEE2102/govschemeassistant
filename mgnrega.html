MGNREGA – Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act

MGNREGA guarantees 100 days of paid work per year to every rural household. Get your MGNREGA Job Card, demand work from your Gram Panchayat, and receive wages in your bank account.

Rural Employment

⚠️ Independent Information Only

This page is written by an independent website. It is not affiliated with the Government of India. Always apply through the official government website linked below.

Simple Overview

MGNREGA is a law — not just a scheme — that guarantees every rural household at least 100 days of paid unskilled manual work per financial year. If work is not provided within 15 days of your demand, you are entitled to an unemployment allowance. Work is mostly local: building roads, digging ponds, planting trees, water conservation. All work must be within 5 km of your home.

Who Can Apply?

  • Any adult member (18+) of a rural household willing to do unskilled manual work
  • No income restriction — any rural household can register
  • Women must make up at least one-third of the workforce in any MGNREGA project

Income Limits

None. MGNREGA is a universal entitlement for all rural households.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card (for all adult household members who want to work)
  • Aadhaar-linked bank or post office account (for wage payment)
  • Recent family photograph
  • Proof of residence in a rural area

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Get a Job Card from your Gram Panchayat

    Visit your Gram Panchayat office and apply for an MGNREGA Job Card. The Panchayat must issue it within 15 days. It is free and your legal right.

  2. 2
    Submit a demand for work

    When you want work, submit a written demand to the Gram Panchayat. Keep a copy of your demand for records.

  3. 3
    Receive work within 15 days

    Work must be provided within 5 km of your village within 15 days of your demand. If not, you are entitled to an unemployment allowance.

  4. 4
    Work and receive wages

    Work at the assigned site. Wages are based on state minimum wage rates and must be paid within 15 days of work completion, directly to your bank/PO account.

  5. 5
    Track your account on nrega.nic.in

    Enter your state, district, and Panchayat on nrega.nic.in to check your work record, payment status, and job card details.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • Guaranteed 100 days of paid work per household per year — a legal right
  • Wages paid per state rates (typically ₹200–₹350 per day)
  • Unemployment allowance if work is not provided within 15 days
  • All work within 5 km of home — no migration needed
  • Permanent community assets created: roads, ponds, canals, plantations

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not getting a Job Card in advance — you must have a card before you can demand work
  • Not keeping a copy of the work demand request
  • Accepting cash wages — wages must go to your bank/PO account. Cash payment is illegal.
  • Not tracking wage payments — if delayed beyond 15 days, you are entitled to compensation

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Can I get more than 100 days of work per year?
The central government guarantees 100 days. Some states offer additional days from their own funds. Ask your Gram Panchayat about the current provision in your state.
Q: My wages are delayed for over a month. What can I do?
You are entitled to delay compensation of 0.05% of wages per day of delay (after 15 days). Raise this in writing with your BDO or lodge a grievance on nrega.nic.in.
Q: Can both husband and wife from the same household work under MGNREGA?
Yes. Multiple adults can work. But the 100-day guarantee is per household — if both work 50 days each, the household has used its full entitlement.

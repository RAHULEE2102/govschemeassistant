PM Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)

PMKVY offers free skill training in 300+ job roles to youth aged 15–45 with a government-recognised certificate. Find a centre near you and enrol today for free.

Skill Development

⚠️ Independent Information Only

This page is written by an independent website. It is not affiliated with the Government of India. Always apply through the official government website linked below.

Simple Overview

Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY) enables young Indians to learn practical job skills — from mobile repairing to healthcare — completely free. After completing training, you receive a government-recognised certificate that improves your employment prospects. Run by the National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC), it covers 300+ job roles across 30+ industry sectors.

Who Can Apply?

  • Indian citizens between 15 and 45 years of age
  • School dropouts, unemployed youth, and those seeking better employment
  • Working professionals who want formal certification through Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL)

Income Limits

No income limit. PMKVY training is free for all eligible applicants.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Educational certificates (Class 8/10/12 mark sheets as applicable)
  • Bank account passbook
  • Passport-size photographs
  • Aadhaar-linked mobile number

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Find a PMKVY training centre

    Visit pmkvyofficial.org or skillindia.gov.in → Find Training Centre. Search by state, district, and job sector.

  2. 2
    Visit the centre and enrol

    Visit the nearest PMKVY-registered training centre with documents. Enrolment is simple and completely free.

  3. 3
    Choose your job role / course

    Select the trade you want to train in. Duration ranges from 3 weeks to 3 months depending on the course.

  4. 4
    Complete the training

    Attend all sessions regularly. Training includes both theory and hands-on practical work.

  5. 5
    Appear for independent assessment

    A third-party assessor — not your training centre — evaluates you at the end. This ensures fair, unbiased results.

  6. 6
    Receive certificate and placement support

    After passing, receive a government-recognised skill certificate. Training centres provide placement assistance to connect you with employers.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • Free training in 300+ job roles — no fees charged
  • Government-recognised certificate recognised by employers nationwide
  • Monetary reward on successful completion in some scheme versions
  • Placement assistance connecting trained candidates with employers
  • RPL certification for those already skilled but without formal proof

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Enrolling with an unregistered centre — verify the centre is officially on the PMKVY website before joining
  • Missing too many training sessions — low attendance may disqualify you from the final assessment
  • Choosing a job role without researching local job market demand in your area

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I am already an electrician but have no certificate. Can PMKVY help?
Yes. PMKVY's Recognition of Prior Learning (RPL) is specifically designed for working people with skills but no formal certificate. You'll be assessed on existing skills.
Q: Does PMKVY guarantee a job after training?
PMKVY does not guarantee a job, but training centres provide placement support and the certificate significantly improves your chances. Many sectors actively recruit PMKVY-certified candidates.

PM Vidya Lakshmi Education Loan Scheme

PM Vidya Lakshmi is a single portal to apply for education loans from multiple banks at once. EWS students (family income up to ₹4.5 lakh) get full interest subsidy during study period.

Education Loan

⚠️ Independent Information Only

This page is written by an independent website. It is not affiliated with the Government of India. Always apply through the official government website linked below.

Simple Overview

PM Vidya Lakshmi allows students to apply for education loans from multiple banks through a single website. You fill one Common Application Form and submit it to multiple banks simultaneously. The scheme also processes interest subsidies under CSIS — for EWS students (family income up to ₹4.5 lakh), the government pays the full loan interest during the study period and moratorium.

Who Can Apply?

  • Indian students admitted to a government-recognised course after Class 12
  • Students admitted to recognised universities for graduation, post-graduation, technical, and professional courses
  • EWS students (family income up to ₹4.5 lakh) for full interest subsidy

Income Limits

For interest subsidy (CSIS): family income up to ₹4.5 lakh per year. Students above this limit can still get loans but without the interest subsidy.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Admission letter from college/university with fee structure
  • Income certificate of parents/guardians
  • Academic mark sheets (Class 10, 12, previous degrees)
  • Bank account details
  • Parent/guardian identity and address proof
  • Collateral documents (for loans above ₹7.5 lakh in some cases)

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Register on vidyalakshmi.co.in

    Create an account as a student using your email ID and mobile number. Enter personal, academic, and course details.

  2. 2
    Fill the Common Education Loan Application Form (CELAF)

    The single form is submitted to all banks simultaneously. Fill it carefully and completely.

  3. 3
    Apply to up to 3 banks

    Search for banks offering loans for your course and loan amount. Apply to up to 3 banks at once.

  4. 4
    Bank contacts you

    The bank(s) will contact you with their specific requirements and terms. You may need to visit the branch.

  5. 5
    Apply for CSIS interest subsidy

    If your family income is below ₹4.5 lakh, apply for the CSIS subsidy through your bank. Government pays all interest during study + moratorium period.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • Single portal to compare and apply to multiple banks — saves time
  • Loan covers tuition, hostel, books, and study expenses
  • CSIS subsidy means no interest accumulates during study period for EWS students
  • Loan amounts from ₹50,000 to ₹1.5 crore depending on bank and course

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying to only one bank — different banks have different approval rates and terms
  • Not separately applying for CSIS interest subsidy after getting the loan
  • Not reading repayment terms carefully before signing the loan agreement

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: Does PM Vidya Lakshmi itself give out loans?
No. The portal connects students with banks. The actual loan is provided by the bank. PM Vidya Lakshmi is only a platform.
Q: When does loan repayment start?
Repayment generally begins 12 months after completing the course, or 6 months after you start working — whichever is earlier. This is the moratorium period.

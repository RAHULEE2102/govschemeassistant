Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)

PM Mudra Yojana provides business loans up to ₹10 lakh to small entrepreneurs without collateral. Three categories — Shishu (₹50K), Kishor (₹5L), Tarun (₹10L) — for any stage of business.

Business Loan

⚠️ Independent Information Only

This page is written by an independent website. It is not affiliated with the Government of India. Always apply through the official government website linked below.

Simple Overview

Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) provides loans to small and micro businesses without requiring collateral (security/guarantee). Available through banks, microfinance institutions, and NBFCs. Three loan categories — Shishu, Kishor, and Tarun — cover startups through established businesses. Farmers, shop owners, artisans, vendors, and service providers all qualify.

Who Can Apply?

  • Any Indian citizen running or wanting to start a micro or small non-farm enterprise
  • Street vendors, rickshaw operators, shop owners, artisans, small manufacturers
  • Women entrepreneurs, SC/ST/OBC entrepreneurs, and rural small businesses

Income Limits

No income limit. Loan is assessed on business viability and credit history.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card and PAN Card
  • Passport-size photographs (2–3)
  • Business proof (shop registration, GST, business address)
  • Bank statement last 6 months (Kishor/Tarun)
  • Proof of SC/ST/OBC category if applicable
  • Business plan / project report (required for Tarun ₹5L+)
  • ITR for last 2 years (Tarun category)

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Visit a bank or MFI branch

    Mudra loans are available at all public/private sector banks, Regional Rural Banks (RRBs), microfinance institutions, and NBFCs. Visit a nearby branch or apply online.

  2. 2
    Fill the Mudra loan application form

    Download from mudra.org.in or get at the bank. Fill personal details, business details, and the loan amount needed.

  3. 3
    Submit documents

    Submit all required documents. For Shishu loans, the process is simpler and faster than for Kishor or Tarun.

  4. 4
    Bank assessment and approval

    Bank assesses your application based on business viability and credit history (CIBIL). Shishu loans can be approved within a few days.

  5. 5
    Receive Mudra Debit Card

    After approval, receive a Mudra Debit Card for flexible fund withdrawals as needed by your business.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • No collateral required — you don't need to pledge land, gold, or property
  • No processing fee for Shishu (up to ₹50,000) loans
  • Loans from ₹10,000 to ₹10 lakh to suit any business size
  • Mudra Card allows flexible withdrawals for working capital
  • Builds a formal credit history for first-time borrowers

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying for Tarun without a solid business plan — applications without detailed project reports are rejected
  • Not having any business registration — even a basic shop registration improves approval chances
  • Poor CIBIL score from past defaults — check your credit score before applying
  • Paying any fees to agents — Mudra loans are free to apply for

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I run a small tea stall. Can I get a Mudra loan?
Yes. A tea stall is a typical micro enterprise eligible for a Shishu or Kishor Mudra loan — for equipment, expansion, or stock purchase.
Q: I took a Shishu loan 2 years ago. Can I get a Kishor loan now?
Yes. If you have been repaying on time, you can apply for a larger Kishor loan to grow your business.

National Scholarship Portal (NSP)

The NSP is a single portal to apply for 50+ central and state government scholarships for SC, ST, OBC, Minority, EWS, and merit students from Class 1 to PhD level.

Simple Overview

The National Scholarship Portal (NSP) is a government platform where students can apply for dozens of central and state government scholarships in one place. Instead of applying separately to each scheme, students register once and apply to multiple scholarships they are eligible for. It is managed by the Ministry of Electronics and Information Technology.

Who Can Apply?

  • Students from Class 1 to PhD level in recognised institutions
  • SC, ST, OBC, Minority community, or EWS students
  • Merit-based scholarship applicants (based on previous class marks)
  • Students with disabilities
  • Institution must be government-recognised and registered on NSP

Income Limits

Pre-Matric: Family income generally below ₹2.5 lakh | Post-Matric SC/ST: below ₹2.5 lakh | Post-Matric OBC: below ₹1 lakh | Minority Pre-Matric: below ₹1 lakh | Merit Cum Means: below ₹2.5 lakh

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card of the student
  • Bank account in the student's own name (Aadhaar-linked) — NOT parents' account
  • Mark sheet of previous class
  • School/college bonafide certificate
  • Income certificate from government authority
  • Caste / community certificate
  • Domicile certificate of the state
  • Passport-size photograph

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Register on scholarships.gov.in

    Go to scholarships.gov.in and click 'New Registration'. Fill in basic details using your Aadhaar. An OTP will be sent to your mobile.

  2. 2
    Complete your profile

    Log in and fill your academic details, family income, bank account, and upload required documents.

  3. 3
    Select applicable scholarship(s)

    From available scholarships, select ones you are eligible for. Check eligibility criteria carefully.

  4. 4
    Submit before the deadline

    Each scholarship has a different deadline. Apply before the closing date — NSP does not accept late applications.

  5. 5
    Institute verification

    Your school or college must verify your application on NSP. Remind the administration to do this within the given timeframe.

  6. 6
    Track and receive payment

    After approval, scholarship amount is credited directly to your bank account. Track status on NSP portal any time.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • One portal for all central and state government scholarships
  • Scholarship amounts range from ₹1,000 to ₹50,000+ per year depending on the scholarship
  • Direct credit to student's bank account
  • Covers fees, examination fees, maintenance, and book grants depending on the scheme

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Applying for a scholarship you are not eligible for — automatic rejection
  • Bank account in parent's name instead of student's name — payment will fail
  • School/college not completing verification on time — follow up regularly
  • Missing application deadline — no extensions given
  • Not renewing the application each year — renewal is required annually

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I received a scholarship last year. Do I apply again?
Yes. NSP scholarships must be renewed every year. Log in before the deadline each year and fill the renewal form with updated academic and income details.
Q: My school isn't on NSP. What do I do?
Ask your principal to register the institution on NSP immediately. Without institutional registration, your application cannot be verified.
Q: Can I apply for more than one scholarship?
Generally one scholarship per student per year. Read each scholarship's terms carefully — applying for multiple simultaneously may disqualify you from both.

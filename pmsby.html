PM Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY)

PMSBY gives ₹2 lakh accident insurance for just ₹20 per year — the most affordable insurance in the world. Available for anyone aged 18–70 with a savings bank account.

Accident Insurance

⚠️ Independent Information Only

This page is written by an independent website. It is not affiliated with the Government of India. Always apply through the official government website linked below.

Simple Overview

Pradhan Mantri Suraksha Bima Yojana (PMSBY) provides accident insurance at just ₹20 per year — one of the cheapest insurance products in the world. If you die in an accident or become permanently disabled, your family receives ₹2 lakh. For partial permanent disability (loss of one hand or one eye), you receive ₹1 lakh. Available to anyone aged 18–70 with a bank account.

Who Can Apply?

  • Indian citizens aged 18 to 70 years
  • Must have a savings bank account
  • Must give auto-debit consent for annual ₹20 premium

Income Limits

No income limit.

Required Documents

  • Savings bank account
  • Aadhaar Card
  • Nominee details

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Visit your bank or use mobile banking

    Go to your bank branch or open the bank's mobile app. Ask for PMSBY enrolment or find it under insurance products.

  2. 2
    Fill the consent form

    Provide nominee's name and relationship. Consent to annual auto-debit of ₹20.

  3. 3
    Done — ₹20 deducted each year

    The ₹20 premium is automatically deducted around 1 June every year. Keep ₹20 in your account on that date.

  4. 4
    Keep your nominee informed

    Tell your family about the policy, nominee name, and how to claim in case of an accident.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • Accident insurance for ₹20/year — cheapest insurance available in India
  • ₹2 lakh for accidental death or total permanent disability
  • ₹1 lakh for partial permanent disability
  • Available for ages 18–70
  • No medical tests required

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not having ₹20 in account on auto-debit date — policy lapses
  • Not informing nominee — family may not know to claim after an accident
  • Confusing PMSBY with PMJJBY — PMSBY is accident-only; PMJJBY covers all deaths

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: What's the difference between PMSBY and PMJJBY?
PMSBY (₹20/year) covers accidental death and disability only. PMJJBY (₹436/year) covers death from any cause. Many people enrol in both for complete coverage at under ₹500/year total.
Q: Can I join both PMSBY and PMJJBY?
Yes, and it is recommended. Together they provide life insurance and accident insurance for about ₹456 per year total.

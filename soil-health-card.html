Soil Health Card Scheme

The Soil Health Card Scheme gives farmers a free card with soil test results and fertiliser recommendations to improve crop yield and reduce unnecessary expenses.

Agriculture

⚠️ Independent Information Only

This page is written by an independent website. It is not affiliated with the Government of India. Always apply through the official government website linked below.

Simple Overview

The Soil Health Card Scheme gives farmers a free 'health card' for their agricultural land. Just like a doctor's prescription, this card tells farmers exactly which nutrients their soil is missing and how much fertiliser to use. This prevents costly overuse of fertilisers and helps grow better crops. Launched in 2015, the scheme aims to test every agricultural plot in India every two years.

Who Can Apply?

  • All farmers who own or cultivate agricultural land
  • No income or land size restrictions
  • Available to all farmers completely free of charge

Income Limits

None. The Soil Health Card is free for all farmers.

Required Documents

  • Aadhaar Card
  • Land ownership proof or a letter from Patwari
  • Mobile number for receiving card digitally

Step-by-Step Application Process

  1. 1
    Contact your Agriculture Department

    Visit your nearest Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) or block-level Agriculture Officer. In most cases, government officials visit villages to collect samples.

  2. 2
    Soil sample collection

    A sample is collected from your field (6 inches depth) by an agriculture officer. You can also submit your own sample as per guidelines.

  3. 3
    Lab testing

    Soil is tested at an approved laboratory for 12 parameters including N, P, K, pH, and micronutrients.

  4. 4
    Receive your card

    The Soil Health Card is sent via SMS to your mobile or delivered through your local agriculture office with specific fertiliser recommendations.

  5. 5
    Follow the recommendations

    Use the card's guidance when purchasing fertiliser for your next crop cycle. Following recommendations can reduce costs and increase yield.

Benefits of the Scheme

  • Free soil testing — saves money compared to private labs
  • Reduces unnecessary fertiliser use (saves significant money)
  • Improves crop yield with the right nutrient balance
  • Contributes to better long-term soil health
  • Can unlock benefits for other agriculture subsidies

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Not using the card's recommendations and continuing old fertiliser habits
  • Getting soil tested only once — renew every 2 years for best results
  • Collecting soil from the wrong depth or wrong part of the field

Frequently Asked Questions

Q: I lost my Soil Health Card. How do I get a new one?
Download a digital copy at soilhealth.dac.gov.in by entering your registered mobile number. You can also contact your local agriculture department.
Q: Will following the recommendations reduce my crop yield?
No. Most farmers find yield increases because the right balance of nutrients is applied. Overuse of one fertiliser can actually reduce yield.
